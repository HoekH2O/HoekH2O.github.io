Hoekwater Development - 2018

Project One
Non-Narrative Video
Video 1: Team Shoot

This project was an immersive experience. Give a timesheet we were tasked with following the instructions and making a video in one take. It was interesting to see how much harder it ended up being. Having to constantly follow the timesheet and translate it into video all at the same time was definitelyinteresting. This video was a great start to this class.

Video 1-1: Introductions Rough Cut

This video focused on us. We had to introduce ourselves by conveying four different moods relative to a location that compliments it at the same time. This video was fairly easy but trying to convey different moods was tricky. The rough cut was soley supposed to be our full introductions combined into a video.

1 1-1 1-1

Video 1-1: Introductions Final Cut

Now for the final cut we were asked to breakdown the time to around 10-15 seconds. So during editing, I basically switched between the different locations I filmed at. This video was fun due the the amount of exploration we did to find the locations.

Video 1-2: Interview Rough Cut

For this project we were asked to interview someone as they tell us a unique story they experienced. It was great to be able to move out of our confort zone and find an interviewee.

Video 1-2: Interview Final Cut

The final cut gave us the opportunity to revisit or perhaps reshoot our interview. Video 1-3: Action Sequence Rough Cut

This video was an adventure to make. We came up with this idea while eating dinner and filmed it right afterwards. It took a while to shoot but ended up being my favorite video to film due to the fun we were having while making it.

Video 1-3: Action Sequence Final Cut

We gained a lot of feedback during the critique. I did the best I could at transfering it over to screen while retaining the purity of the roough cut. The video gave me a lot of insight on how much planning goes into making an action movie. It was a great experience.

Video 1-4: Non-Narrative Transition Rough Cut

This video was short and simple. We were tasked with seraching for found footage that we could intertwine with our own footage to make fluent transitions. It was hard finding the footage and planning a way to make them come together, but was fun to film and see it come together in the end.

Video 1-4: Non-Narrative Transition Final Cut

After the critique, I did some minor changes to how the video switches to each shot. I added some subtle interactivity with the music playing in the backgound. The video was fun to make and was worth seeing how it all came together in the end.
