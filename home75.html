Video 2-2: Where Cameras Don't Belong Mount System

This mount took some planning. I started with my camera case. I knew I wanted to use a fish eye lens, so using the camera case was already a final choice. Figuring out how to mount the ear bud was another issue. I ended up using about 10 rubber bands, one paper clip, two pieces of clear tape, and three pens. I created a ledge for the paper clip to rest on with the pens as i taped it down. I eased the earbud on the paper clip concealing the metal behind the chord. Lastly, I squeezed my hand in the rubber bands as they acted as my handle, then with some awkward arms movements, the mount looked somewhat convincing.

Video 2-3: Phase 2 - Rough Cut Surveillance Video Presentation

Artist Statement:

How much noise can you hear in the library? We asked this question to understand the different levels within each floor of the library here at SJSU. We are told that each floor is supposed to be more quiet as we reach the top, and we set out to examine how each floor varies in sound. We found that the floors don't necessarily get louder as we go lower, but rather vary due to population and often popularity. The forth floor or "Spartan Floor" has the most students and it is the loudest floor in the library, mostly due to the amount of students and the volume they communicate at... Continue Reading

Artist Statement:

While the students aren't intentionally being loud, the amount of students decides how loud the overall floor becomes. We continue down and come to find that the next floors aren't at all louder in any sense. That the constant traffic of people and movement from one point to the next is the only distinguishing sound. We came to understand the theory of volume controlled floors, ultimately drawing the conclusion that each floor may have a suggested volume, but is inconsistent due to the population of students and the amount of sounds they make during that time of day. Video 2-3: Phase 2 - Final Cut Surveillance Video Presentation

Process:

To demonstrate our creative question, we took it upon ourselves to film every floor in the library and record video and audio to ultimately distinguish the audio differences on each floor. We used the elevator shot and numbers to present the floor location, and the "middle" of each room for our main shot. After the critique, we applied the advice of our peers and shortened the clip. It originally stood at around 2:39, I sped up the clips to make more efficient use of the audience's time. Less time in the elevator and more time on the floor.
